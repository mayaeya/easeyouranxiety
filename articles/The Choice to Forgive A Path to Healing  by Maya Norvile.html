"The Choice to Forgive: A Path to Healing" by Maya Eya

Forgiveness is not easy. Especially when you’ve been hurt deeply. But holding on to that hurt can quietly drain you. It sits in your body, in your thoughts, and slowly turns into stress, anxiety, or heaviness. Forgiveness is not about saying “it was okay.” It wasn’t. It’s about choosing to stop carrying it. When you forgive, you are not doing it for the other person. You are doing it for yourself. So you can breathe again. So your mind can rest.

What forgiveness really means.Forgiveness means letting go of the need to punish. Letting go of replaying the same situation again and again in your head. It doesn’t mean you trust again. It doesn’t mean you allow the same behaviour again. It just means: “I release this from me.” If you believe in God, you can see forgiveness as giving the situation back to Him. You don’t have to carry justice on your own.

What happens when you don’t forgive. Unforgiveness can feel like holding something heavy for too long. At first, it feels justified. But over time, it becomes exhausting. It can show up as tension, overthinking, anger, or sadness that doesn’t fully go away. Many people try to ignore it, push it down, or explain it away. But it usually stays.

A different way. Instead of pushing it away, try to notice the pain. Acknowledge it. It’s real. And then slowly, in your own time, choose to loosen your grip on it. You don’t have to do it all at once.

Forgiveness can be quiet. It doesn’t have to be a big moment. Sometimes it’s just a small thought: “I don’t want to carry this anymore.” If you pray, you can bring it there. Not with perfect words. Just honestly.

Make it a gentle habit. Feelings don’t disappear in one day. Forgiveness can come in layers. A little at a time. Each time you choose to let go, even a bit, you make space for more peace.

In the end... You don’t forgive because they deserve it. You forgive because you deserve peace and that’s enough 🤍
