"The Choice to Forgive: A Path to Healing" by Maya Šustickienė

Forgiveness is more than just a spiritual practice; it's a powerful antidote to the emotional burdens of stress, anxiety, and depression. When we forgive, we restore not only our relationships with God and others but also the relationship we have with ourselves. This healing process is crucial for those struggling with inner turmoil, as it helps release the heavy weight that unforgiveness places on our minds and hearts. As the Bible reminds us, "For if you forgive others their trespasses, your heavenly Father will also forgive you, but if you do not forgive others their trespasses, neither will your Father forgive your trespasses." (Matthew 6:14-15).

Forgiveness: A Divine Path

To forgive is to release punishment, to stop the execution of judgment, and to halt the harvest of a bad seed. Only God has the authority to pardon punishment if He so chooses. Our calling and responsibility is to forgive. This act of forgiveness is not just about letting go of grudges; it's about breaking the cycle of negativity that unforgiveness breeds in our lives.

Forgiveness allows us to step into God’s divine plan, serving as vessels through which His love and mercy can flow into even the most hardened hearts. We are, in essence, God’s pathway into the hearts of His stubborn children. By holding firmly to our Lord Jesus Christ and following His example of forgiving love, we embody the grace that can transform not only our own lives but also the lives of those who have wronged us.

The Consequences of Unforgiveness

Unforgiveness is a poison that contaminates both our lives and the lives of others, leading to spiritual, mental, and emotional darkness. The secret of unforgiveness is that many Christians know they need to forgive, but very few truly understand how to do it. This is especially true for those who struggle with anxiety and depression, where the emotional toll of unforgiveness can exacerbate their condition, trapping them in a cycle of pain and resentment.

When people refuse to forgive, they often attempt to resolve matters of the heart by suppressing their feelings or reasoning them away. They may deny their emotions, attempt to rationalize them, or bury them deep inside. However, these strategies often backfire, leading to more stress, anxiety, and emotional pain. True forgiveness requires us to acknowledge our pain and then choose to release it. It’s not about denying the hurt but about finding a way to move beyond it.

The Role of the Holy Spirit

The Holy Spirit plays a crucial role in guiding us toward forgiveness. When the Holy Spirit illuminates our sins and the hurts we’ve caused or received, it often brings a sense of unrest. This discomfort is not meant to harm us but to lead us toward repentance and healing. By acknowledging our hidden sins and the pain we've buried, we open the door to God’s healing grace.

Righteousness and peace come when we learn to repent and forgive, allowing the Holy Spirit to cleanse our hearts. This process can be challenging, especially when dealing with deep-seated hurts, but it is essential for our spiritual and emotional well-being.

Forgiveness as a Daily Practice

Forgiveness isn't a one-time event but a daily practice. Discipline in forgiveness means consistently recognizing and addressing our pain, disappointment, and grievances as they arise. This discipline is essential for those who struggle with anxiety and depression, as it helps to prevent negative emotions from building up and overwhelming us. By turning to God in prayer—seeking understanding, courage, wisdom, and relief—we find the strength to forgive and, in turn, find peace.

Silent prayer is particularly valuable in this process because it allows us to respond instantly to our needs. When we are hurt, our natural response is often to suppress our feelings or try to rationalize them, but these strategies can backfire, leading to more stress and emotional pain. Instead, we should acknowledge our pain immediately and turn to God for guidance and healing.

Finding Peace Through Repentance and Forgiveness

Many people, especially those dealing with hidden struggles, may find that unresolved hurt manifests as inner turmoil, which can further fuel anxiety and depression. The Holy Spirit can bring these hidden pains to light, not to cause distress, but to offer a pathway to healing through repentance and forgiveness.

For a deeper exploration of how forgiveness can be a vital part of your journey to emotional and mental wellness, you might find the book Choosing Forgiveness by John Loren Sandford, Paula Sandford, and Lee Bowman to be a valuable resource. It offers practical advice and spiritual wisdom for those seeking to heal from the inside out.
