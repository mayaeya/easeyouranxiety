"When Love Was Never Real 💔" by Maya Eya

I didn’t know how to start this. Not because there are no words — but because there are too many.

This kind of pain isn’t just heartbreak. It’s the moment you realise the relationship you believed in wasn’t what you thought it was.

At the beginning, it felt real. There was hope. After everything before, letting someone in felt like a risk — but also like something good. You trust what they say. You believe what they show you.

And then, slowly, things change.

The same person who made you feel safe starts making you feel small. Conversations turn cold. Respect disappears. You’re left trying to understand what went wrong, while they act like nothing ever really mattered.

At some point, you stop asking what happened and start asking something worse — was any of it real? That question stays.

Not because you’re weak, but because you were genuine. You showed up honestly. You cared. You tried to build something real.

And when that isn’t returned, it doesn’t just hurt your feelings. It affects how you see yourself. It brings doubt. It feeds anxiety. It makes you question your own value.

But here’s the part that’s easy to miss when you’re in it: Someone treating you badly doesn’t define your worth. It shows their limits, not yours.

You can still care about what you thought you had. You can still miss it. That doesn’t make you foolish — it makes you human.

What matters is recognising what was real and what wasn’t. Your effort was real. Your feelings were real.

The rest? Not something you can fix alone.

There’s no clean ending to this kind of story. No quick way to move on. Just small steps — getting through the day, then the next one.

And slowly, without forcing it, you start to come back to yourself.

Not the version shaped by someone else — but the one that existed before all of this.

That’s where things begin again.🖤
