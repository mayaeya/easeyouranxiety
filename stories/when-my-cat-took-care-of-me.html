"When My Cat Took Care of Me🐈‍⬛" by Maya Eya

I got my cat when he was around six months old.🐾 From the beginning, he wasn’t like other cats. He didn’t purr. He didn’t play. He didn’t sit on my lap. He didn’t like people, noises, or even bright light. He was always a bit scared, always distant. He got used to me in his own way. He would sleep on a chair in my bedroom or in the bed next to my feet but not closer. Sometimes he would let me hold him for a short moment, but if it was too much, he would hiss or scratch. He liked space. That was just who he was.

And then… everything changed. After the event in my life that brought depression, my cat became different. He lived with me for about a year now, and first time he came and tried to sit on my lap... for the first time. He didn’t even know how to do it properly. I had to guide him a little. And then he just sat there, purring. He started sleeping next to my head. He was purring a lot. He followed me everywhere — to the kitchen, to the bathroom, always next to me. He never did that before. He stayed close, even when there were noises that used to scare him. Instead of hiding, he would look at me.

But the most surprising thing… he was putting me to sleep.💤 At first, I didn’t think much of it. He would lie next to my head, purring, and I would slowly fall asleep. But one night, I couldn’t fall asleep so easily. I was lying there, and suddenly I noticed — he stopped purring. He lifted his head and looked at me. He saw my eyes were still open. Then he lay down again and started purring. This happened a few times. I finally fell asleep. After few days I decided to test him. I pretended to sleep. Night like ever night that time, my cat lie next to my head, purring... He stopped purring. I could feel him watching me. Waiting. When I didn’t move, after a while… he left. He was checking if I was asleep.

Now he doesn’t do that anymore. For a long time, he stayed close to me. Always with me. Always watching. Now, he is different again. He doesn’t sleep next to my head anymore. If I try, he hisses and runs away. He’s back to being that same distant, sensitive cat — hiding under the bed, afraid of noises. And somehow… I know I’m okay now. Because he doesn’t feel he need to take care of me anymore.🤍
