"Fighting Depression Alone: Part 11 - When “I’m Fine” Is Too Heavy" by Maya Eya

There are days when life feels like a performance. Wake up, get dressed, smile, talk. 😶‍🌫️

You go out, you meet people, you talk. You keep the smile. You keep the “I’m alright.” You keep the mask on because it feels easier than explaining how you really feel. But after a while, it gets heavy.

You start feeling tired from the smiling. There comes a point when you don’t want to see anyone. You don’t want to talk. You want to disappear from the constant “be social, be active.”

People love to look from the outside and make their own stories. They think your life is easy. They imagine you sitting at home doing nothing, relaxing, chilling.

If only they knew. 🙄

Lately I’ve been trying to live more like a “normal” person. More activities. More going out. More communication. More smiling. And everyone sees that and thinks, “Oh, she’s fine now. Maya is okay.” But no. I’m not suddenly cured. My anxiety didn’t pack a suitcase and move out.

Inside, I want to be in my room with my phone off, door closed, no noise, no pressure, no people. 🕯️

People think I’m free. They don’t see me studying. And because everything is remote, they assume it’s nothing, they think I’m not doing anything. As if the knowledge just magically appears in my head. Honestly, try learn something new first — then talk about “easy”.

They don’t see me writing all my appointments and meetings in my calendar. Too many things. Too many demands.

They don’t see that social interactions drain me. Yes, I meet people. Yes, I talk. But I’m constantly pushing myself, even when I’m not ok.

And the worst part — people judge from a distance. “Oh, she’s doing nothing.” If only I could give them my brain for one day. They wouldn’t last two hours. 😤

Sometimes all I want is silence. Just a break from being “fine”.

And honestly? Sometimes we need silence more than anything. You’re allowed to choose yourself. 🌿
