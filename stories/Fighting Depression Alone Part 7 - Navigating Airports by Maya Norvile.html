"Fighting Depression Alone: Part 7 - Navigating Airports" by Maya Eya

Today, I flew back from my holiday. It didn’t feel like returning to normal life. It felt like falling from something calm and peaceful straight back into reality.

My anxiety doesn’t take breaks. It turns simple things into something heavy. The world feels too fast, too loud, too confusing. And today, like so many days, I felt useless. I hadn’t really done anything, yet I was completely exhausted.

All I wanted was to sleep. But the moment I arrived at the airport, the dizziness hit. Everything became blurry. It felt like the walls were closing in. Breathing became harder. My mind went foggy. I couldn’t even find the entrance. My heart was racing, not from excitement, but from fear. The kind of fear that freezes you in place.

This time, I had to face it alone.

Airports are overwhelming even on a good day. When your mind is already working against you, it becomes something else entirely. Every sign feels confusing. Every step feels too much. Even standing still feels wrong, like everyone is watching, even if they’re not.

But here is the thing — I was still there. I still showed up. I still got on that plane.

And something important happened. Airport staff came to help me. They guided me through step by step when I felt completely lost. It made me realise how many people might be struggling like this but don’t know that help like this even exists.

You can ask for assistance at the airport. You don’t have to explain everything. You don’t have to prove how bad it is. If you feel overwhelmed, anxious, or confused — that is enough.

We don’t talk about this enough. So many people are quietly struggling through things that are much harder than they look.

Today was hard. Really hard. But I still did it. And maybe that counts more than anything else.

If you’re dealing with this too — there is help, even in places you wouldn’t expect.✈️💙
